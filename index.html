What is Parallax Scrolling Effect?

  • Parallax scrolling is a special scrolling technique used in web design where background images throughout a web page move slower than foreground images, creating an illusion of depth on a two-dimensional site.It is one of the most used design techniques in web design right now, but also something you probably donâ€™t even realize you have already experienced.

  • Parallax is based on optical illusion.Since the human eye perceives objects that are close to us as larger than things farther away,we perceive distant object as if they were moving slowly.The illusion has been long adopted into parallax across diffrent mediums,fostering a realisticcs effect.It's first use was in traditional animation such as Disney's Snow White,Super Mario etc.With Advancements in HTML and CSS,parallax effects later evolved into the world of Web Design we know today.

Benefits of Parallax Effect in Web Design

  • Parallax websites increase visual storytelling

    Users' attention span are getting shorter due to abundance of content available.Hence companies are turning towards visual storytelling.It holds the users' attention for longer and fosters a deeper connection with your brand as it connects with the user on a different level due to it being visually appealing and it can be an excellent way to integrate storytelling into your website.

  • Parallax Websites reduce bounce rate

    Building a connection with the user using parallax effect(if executed properly) can reduce the bounce rate. Bounce rate refers to the rate at which the user navigates away from your website.Parallax Scrolling naturally encourages users to continue scrolling and scanning the page down below the fold.
